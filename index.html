Welcome, I'm Henry! I'm currently a postdoctoral research associate and lecturer in the ECE department at Princeton University. My research lies at the intersection of machine learning and wireless communications and networking.

My work follows two main directives: (i) federated learning over and for wireless networks and (ii) AI-native wireless communications and its implications for 6G and Next Generation wireless systems. On the federated learning side, I analyze the varied relationships across distributed edge and fog devices/clients, spanning hardware, data statistics, and physical /wireless links, to optimize their joint development of machine intelligence for modern IoT, communication, and edge/fog networks.
