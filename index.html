Welcome, I'm Henry! I'm currently a postdoctoral research associate and lecturer in the ECE department at Princeton University. My research lies at the intersection of machine learning and wireless communications and networking.

I am also passionate about teaching and education, more generally, having recently received the Princeton Engineering Council's Excellence in Teaching Award for my work in EGR 151 - Foundations of Engineering: Mechanics, Energy, and Wavess.

At Princeton, I'm advised by Mung Chiang and H. Vincent Poor. Previously, I earned my Ph.D. in Dec. 2023 at Purdue University, West Lafayette, advised by Chris Brinton. I also earned my B.S.E.E. (with distinction) from Purdue University, West Lafayette in Dec. 2018. Go Boilermakers!
