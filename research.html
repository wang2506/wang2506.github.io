Research

Overview

Specifically, my work brings distributed and federated learning into the wireless layer, with the goal of making machine learning (and intelligence more generally) a native capability of 6G/NextG systems. My work first establishes the foundation by getting machine learning, both training and inference, to effectively operate over practical networks. These networks suffer from pervasive heterogeneity, spanning hardware, data, wireless links, and goals. Standard federated approaches simplify certain aspects, but realistic IoT and edge deployments cannot. I then build on this foundation to create novel learning-native wireless capabilities, such as semantic communications and intelligent beamforming for future communication systems.

The following sections outline the three key directions that I take towards realizing my vision of federated intelligence for AI-native wireless communications. Each section also highlights selected publications. For a full list of publications, see here.

Research Thread

Distributed & Federated Intelligence
over Heterogeneous Edge/Fog Networks

%%% what it is %%% what you've done

Distributed and federated approaches have become popular techniques to enable both the training of and inference from (hence, intelligence) ML models across sets of computation and communication capable devices. These devices vary widely in local capabilities, e.g., data distributions, compute power, and/or ratio of labeled to unlabeled data, all of which lead to complex group dynamics. To enable wireless networks to benefit from such intelligence then, it is iumportant to carefully control and optimize both training and inference stages with For example, extensive data distribution heterogeneity can lead to training divergence in federated learning. For wireless networks Thus, distributed and especially federated intelligence that spans both training and inference stages, is foundational to my vision. Before machine intelligence can live inside a wireless system, it has to survive the network: unreliable links, limited communication budgets, and devices whose hardware, data, and goals all differ. This heterogeneity is something standard distributed and federated approaches simplify away, but that realistic edge, fog, and IoT deployments cannot avoid. My doctoral and postdoctoral work develops the theory and algorithms that make distributed learning work under these conditions — treating it as foundational infrastructure for learning-native networks rather than an end in itself.

This thread spans network-aware optimization of distributed learning, device sampling and resource optimization for heterogeneous federated learning, online and personalized learning over multi-tiered networks, and server placement for vertical FL in dynamic edge/fog networks. More recent work extends these methods toward serverless and decentralized settings, and toward the robustness of learning-based wireless components — for example, defending federated signal classifiers against evasion attacks.

Research Thread

Learning-Native Wireless Communications (6G / NextG)

Most machine learning for wireless treats the communication system as fixed and the model as a black box. I take the opposite view: that the structure of a wireless problem — its physics, its optimization landscape, its distributed nature — should shape the learning architecture itself. My work here designs learning methods that are native to the communication system rather than generic, using distributed and unrolled optimization to build new physical- and network-layer capabilities.

A representative direction is distributed Airy beamforming via federated network unrolling (ongoing work), where beamforming optimization is cast as a learned, unrollable procedure distributed across the network — a concrete bridge between the distributed-learning tools I've developed and the wireless PHY. Related work includes holographic beamforming for semantic communications, which explores how learning can reshape what a communication system chooses to transmit in the first place.

Recent & Ongoing

Recent & Ongoing Directions

Alongside these two threads, I work with students on emerging directions at the edge of learning and wireless systems: communication-efficient multimodal federated learning, controllable LLM offloading and routing for edge deployment, federated unlearning for generative models, and low-cost RF fingerprinting via TinyML. Several of these are led by undergraduate and graduate researchers I mentor.
